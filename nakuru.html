About Lake Nakuru

Lake Nakuru is one of Kenya's most famous lakes, located in the heart of the Great Rift Valley. It is known for its stunning birdlife, including the famous flamingos that often cover the shores of the lake in a vibrant pink blanket.

The lake is situated in the Lake Nakuru National Park, which is home to diverse wildlife such as giraffes, rhinos, buffaloes, and lions. The park also features beautiful landscapes and is a UNESCO biosphere reserve.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Lake Nakuru is during the dry season from July to October. The weather is more favorable for game viewing, and the flamingos are a common sight around the lake.

Things to Do in Lake Nakuru

  • Game drive in Lake Nakuru National Park
  • Visit the baboon cliff for a scenic view of the lake
  • Watch the flamingos and other bird species
  • Hike to the waterfall within the park
  • Visit the rhino sanctuary within the park

Travel Tips

  • Wear comfortable walking shoes for exploring the park.
  • Carry binoculars for bird watching and wildlife spotting.
  • Bring sunscreen and a hat, as the park can get hot during the day.
  • Respect the wildlife and maintain a safe distance during game drives.

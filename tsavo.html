About Tsavo National Park

Tsavo National Park, located in southeastern Kenya, is one of the largest national parks in the country. The park is divided into Tsavo East and Tsavo West, both offering breathtaking landscapes, diverse wildlife, and unique attractions like the Mzima Springs and the famous Tsavo Lions.

Tsavo is home to the “Big Five” and is known for its large populations of elephants, making it a must-visit for wildlife enthusiasts and safari lovers.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Tsavo is during the dry season from June to October when the wildlife is easier to spot as animals gather around waterholes.

Things to Do in Tsavo

  • Go on a safari to see the Big Five
  • Visit Mzima Springs and see hippos and crocodiles
  • Explore the Yatta Plateau, the longest lava flow in the world
  • Relax by the Voi River
  • Take a nature walk with a local guide

Travel Tips

  • Always have a local guide when venturing into the park
  • Carry enough water as the park can be quite hot
  • Wear neutral-colored clothing to blend in with the environment
  • Book your safari in advance to secure the best deals

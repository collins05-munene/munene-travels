About Mount Kenya

Mount Kenya is the highest mountain in Kenya and the second-highest in Africa, after Mount Kilimanjaro. A UNESCO World Heritage site, it offers a unique mix of alpine vegetation, glaciers, and wildlife.

The mountain is a popular destination for trekkers, with several routes to the summit, and is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna, including elephants, buffaloes, and rare birds.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Mount Kenya is between January and March, and then again from June to September. These months offer the most stable weather, ideal for hiking and trekking.

Things to Do at Mount Kenya

  • Climb to the summit of Mount Kenya
  • Go on a safari in Mount Kenya National Park
  • Visit the alpine lakes like Lake Michaelson
  • Experience the breathtaking views from the peaks
  • Bird watching and wildlife safaris

Travel Tips

  • Ensure you are well-prepared with hiking gear and clothes suitable for cold climates.
  • Acclimatize before attempting to climb the mountain to avoid altitude sickness.
  • Hire a local guide for a safer and more informative hiking experience.
  • Stay hydrated and bring high-energy snacks during your trek.
